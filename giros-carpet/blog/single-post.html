Clogged dryer vents are a leading cause of home fires in the United States. The U.S. Fire Administration reports that dryers cause approximately 2,900 residential fires every year, resulting in an estimated five deaths, 100 injuries, and $35 million in property damages. A significant portion of these fires is due to lint buildup in dryer vents.

Dryers are incredibly convenient appliances that have become a staple in most households. They save us time and effort by quickly drying our clothes, but what many homeowners don't realize is that they also come with an often overlooked fire hazard—clogged dryer vents. This hidden danger not only poses a risk to your home but can also lead to thousands of dollars in damages.

Lint is the enemy here. When you use your dryer, tiny fibers and lint particles shed from your clothes. While your dryer's lint trap does its best to capture these, some lint manages to bypass the trap and gets trapped in the dryer vent. Over time, this lint buildup can restrict airflow and become a highly flammable hazard.

Remember, when it comes to your dryer, a little maintenance today can prevent a lot of trouble tomorrow. Your clothes will dry faster, and you'll sleep easier knowing your home is safe from this hidden threat.

Burning Smell: A burning odor while using the dryer is a clear warning sign. Lint in the vent can ignite and produce a distinct, unpleasant smell.

Overheating Dryer: A dryer that feels excessively hot to the touch is a red flag. Clogs can cause your dryer to overheat, increasing the risk of a fire.

Drying Takes Longer: One of the first signs of a clogged dryer vent is that your clothes will take longer to dry. Reduced airflow means your dryer has to work harder and longer to get the job done.
